Projects

NoVAGraphS: Non-Visual Access to Graphical Structures

I am currently involved in the NoVAGraphS project, which focuses on improving accessibility for visually impaired students in STEM education. This research is conducted in collaboration with Laboratorio Polin, with the goal of designing innovative tools and methodologies for accessing graphical structures non-visually.

Within this project, I work on accessible educational dialogue systems, with a particular focus on how natural language interaction can support the exploration and understanding of formal and graphical structures.

Perspective-Aware Dialogue Systems

I also work on Understanding, Generation and Management of Pragmatically Constrained Utterances in Dialogue Systems through Perspective-Aware Language Modeling, a research project carried out in collaboration with Amazon Alexa.

My work focuses on the role of perspective, pragmatics, and speaker-aware language variation in dialogue systems. In particular, I investigate how language models can better understand and generate utterances that are appropriate to a given communicative context, user perspective, and pragmatic constraint.

This line of research includes the analysis and generation of irony, rhetorical figures, and stylistically or pragmatically constrained dialogue. I contribute to the design of datasets, prompting and evaluation pipelines, and computational methods for modelling how different perspectives influence dialogue generation and interpretation.
