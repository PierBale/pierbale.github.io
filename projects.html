Projects

Projects connecting computational linguistics with accessibility, personalisation and human-centred AI.

NoVAGraphS: Non-Visual Access to Graphical Structures

I am involved in NoVAGraphS, a project focused on improving accessibility for visually impaired students in STEM education. The research is conducted in collaboration with Laboratorio Polin and explores innovative ways of accessing graphical structures non-visually.

Within the project, I work on accessible educational dialogue systems, investigating how natural-language interaction can support the exploration and understanding of formal and graphical structures.

Perspective-Aware Dialogue Systems

I work on Understanding, Generation and Management of Pragmatically Constrained Utterances in Dialogue Systems through Perspective-Aware Language Modeling, a research project carried out in collaboration with Amazon Alexa.

My work focuses on perspective, pragmatics and speaker-aware language variation. I investigate how language models can better understand and generate utterances appropriate to a communicative context, user perspective and pragmatic constraint.

This research includes irony, rhetorical figures and stylistically constrained dialogue, as well as the design of datasets, prompting strategies and human-centred evaluation methods.
